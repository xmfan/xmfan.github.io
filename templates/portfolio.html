watchr

Built at HackPrinceton 2016, I was saddened by the terror incidents in Europe and Asia and I wanted to take a shot at crowdsourcing public security. The gist of it was to use everyone's devices connected to the same public network (those locations being more likely to be targeted), and have them log abnormally loud noises (i.e.: bombings, shootings, screams, etc.) to a central server. The server would then cluster the logs by location, trace the epicenter of the incident, and issue evacuation instructions to the devices, personalized to their locations.

Before this project, I felt somewhat jaded. This served me as a reminder of the potential impact I can have on the perceived world.
